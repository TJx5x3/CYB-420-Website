Introduction

In an era where digital landscapes shape global power dynamics, understanding and addressing cyber threats has become critical. This website delves into Iran's comprehensive cyber warfare strategy, outlining the challenges, objectives, and key players shaping the nation’s cyber defenses and offensive capabilities. Explore insights into operatives, threat groups, personnel strategies, and Iran's overarching mission to secure and expand its influence in cyberspace.


The Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps was established in April of 1979. We have several cyber divisions operating under our name. The Electronic Warfare and Cyber Defense Organization (EWCD), the Intelligence Organization (IO), Cyber-Electronic Command (CEC), and the Basij Cyber Council. We are led by Hossein Salami and operate under the Supreme leader.


The IRGC is committed to defending the Islamic Republic’s values, ideology, and sovereignty. In an era of rapidly evolving threats, we are at the front of defending Iran from adversaries who seek to undermine its sovereignty. The IRGC protects both the physical and digital boarders of Iran. In addition to defending our cyberspace, the IRGC’s offensive cyber capabilities enables to deter and neutralize adversaries who attempt to harm our national interests through cyber warfare.


We have many talents working within our agency. We recruit anyone who has the skills that can benefit our cause. Our espionage and sabotage groups are in need of more operatives to help our mission. We may be in competition with the Ministry of Intelligence; however, we do collaborate when needed.
