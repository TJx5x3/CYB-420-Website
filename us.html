Federal Cyber Laws:


  • Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA):
    • Enacted in 1986
    • Amends Comprehensive Crime Control Act of 1984
      • First major cybercrime legislation and was not very effective
    • Prohibits intentionally accessing a computer without authorization or exceeding authorization
    • Has been amended 9 times between 1988 and 2008
    • Amendments Include:
      • Criminalizing distribution of malicious code
      • Criminalizing denial of service attacks
      • Criminalizing accessing, tampering, and attempted extortion
      • Criminalizing trafficking in passwords or other access control mechanisms
      • Expanding protection to private computers
      • Including punishing taking information off of systems
      • Allowing civil action to be taken
        • Now companies can threaten use of this act to prevent others from leaking private information
        • But can also get judgement easier when evidence isn’t rock solid because proof doesn’t need to be “beyond a reasonable doubt”

    • Cybersecurity Information Sharing Act (CISA):

      • Passed in 2015
      • Key Terms:
        • Cyber Threat Indicators: necessary information to identify threats and information on actual or potential harm as a result of a cyber threat
        • Defense Measures: something that detects, prevents, or mitigates a known or suspected cyber threat or security vulnerability
        • Cybersecurity Purpose: purpose of protecting an information system or information from a cyber threat or security vulnerability
      • Encourages private companies to voluntarily share information by allowing federal antitrust exemptions, immunity from federal/state disclosure laws, and a non-waiver of applicable protections for sharing materials
      • Before, companies didn't share cyber threats for fear of violating regulations so this act offers protections so companies will disclose vulnerabilities to improve overall cybersecurity
      • Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) becomes the central hub for sharing of defensive measures and cyber threat indicators between private and public sectors
