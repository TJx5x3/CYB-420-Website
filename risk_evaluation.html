Risk Evaluation


Threat 1: Artificial Intelligence

Overview:

As AI becomes more reliable and better at collecting information, AI may allow malicious actors to automate and personalize social engineering strategies, which would make them more convincing and harder to detect what is real and what is fake.

Examples of Attack Vectors:

  • Deepfakes: AI-generated audio, video, or images that convincingly impersonate trusted individuals.

  • Advanced Phishing: Highly personalized phishing emails or messages generated using natural language processing (NLP)

  • AI chatbot Exploitation: Chatbots within the company may become capable of gathering and extracting sensitive information and collecting personal information on employees within the organization.

Mitigation Strategies:

  • Advanced Training Programs: Provide interactive security awareness training that covers AI-driven threats like deepfakes and conversational AI tools.

  • Communication Verification: Use multi-layered authentication systems for sensitive communications and verbal passwords that change daily in case of emergency.

  • Implement Strong Data Privacy Measures Reduce the quantity of personal data published publicly and on company websites, limiting the information accessible for AI algorithms to exploit.

  • Collaboration: By working with industry partners and threat intelligence platforms we can keep informed about emerging AI-enabled attack vectors and create remediation plans to avoid compromise.


Threat 2: Cloud Security

Overview:

Cloud Security threats are growing as organizations are increasingly moving operations to the cloud.

Examples of Attack Vectors:

  • Data Breaches

  • Cloud Misconfiguration:

    • Attackers can exploit misconfigurations in security settings. This includes user access control, out-of-date software, etc.

  • Cloud Malware Injection:

    • Add malicious service modules or invected VMs.

    • Utilizing compromised user accounts to inject malware

  • Insecure APIs:

    • APIS can be exploited to gain sensitive data.

      Attackers can obtain data through injection attacks if inputs are not sanitized.

Mitigation Strategies:
  • Use strong encryption methods for data.
  • Scan for misconfigurations.
  • Implement zero-trust and least privilege architectures.
  • Make sure API inputs and outputs are properly sanitized.
  • Use proper cloud identity management practices.
  • Conduct regular cloud security assessments.
