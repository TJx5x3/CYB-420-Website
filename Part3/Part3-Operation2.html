Operation 2:

Israeli Water Infrastructure Attack

Description:

  • Occurred in April of 2020.
  • Linked to Iranian group: CyberAv3ngers
  • In two rural districts, Iranian hackers attacked computers that control water flow and wastewater treatments.
  • Attack attempted to poison the water by increasing chlorine levels in water going to residential areas.
  • Also sought to trigger fail-safe which would shut down pumps to leave thousands without water during heatwave.
  • Pumps went into continuous operation, wiped out information, irregularities due to unplanned changes in data.
  • Ultimately no damage was done (minor issues in local water systems).
  • Israel retaliated by launching a cyberattack on an Iranian port, attempting to harm the economy.
  • Was first modern attack on civilian infrastructure from Iran to Israel.

Offensive Cyber Strategy:

  • Logical:
    • Exploited Passwords:
      • Many facilities use default passwords so it was easy for hackers to gain access.
    • Exploited known vulnerabilities:
      • Hackers are able to manipulate systems remotely by gaining access through devices with unpatched vulnerabilities.
      • Scanned for devices to find these vulnerabilities.
  • Physical:
    • Manipulated programmable logic controllers to increase chlorine levels and prevent water from being distributed.
    • Program logic controllers are physical devices in the facility that are essential to automate processes in water treatment and distribution systems.
    • Able to physically attack remotely.
  • Summary of related tools:
    • Spear-phishing:
      • Attempt to trick specific individuals into providing sensitive information or downloading malware typically through emails that appear legitimate
    • Lateral Movement:
      • Strategy used by hackers to navigate from one compromised system to gain access to another, more valuable target.
    • Exploiting zero-day vulnerabilities:
      • Exploiting vulnerabilities that the developer is unaware of to gain access to a system before a patch is released
    • Use of encrypted communication channels to obfuscate command-and-control operations:
      • Conceal communication between malware on a compromised system and the attacker’s command server

Defensive Cyber Strategy:

  • Logical:
    • Intrusion Detection and Prevention Systems:
      • Monitor network traffic in real-time to detect suspicious behavior or any unauthorized access attempts.
      • This should be implemented as a high priority for any systems directly controlling critical infrastructure.
      • Patterns of malware can be detected and blocked before they reach operational systems.
    • Multi-Factor Authentication:
      • Secure access to critical infrastructure control systems and administrative tools to ensure that access cannot be easily gained.
      • Additional verification, such as a onetime passcode or biometrics, is required to gain access instead of using just a username and password.
      • Credentials can still be stolen, but it is harder to gain access due to the second method of authorization required.
  • Physical:
    • Air-gapped systems:
      • Critical infrastructure control systems isolate from the internet and other networks to prevent remote access.
      • Iran was able to gain control through remote access – this could prevent similar future attacks.
      • A physical attack (such as a USB drop) would be the only way to attack this type of system.
      • Protects against malware, ransomware, zero-day exploits, advanced persistent threats.
      • Access controls and authentication must be strict and data is transferred manually.
  • Summary of Related Tools:
    • Firewalls
      • Barrier between trusted internal networks and untrusted external networks
      • Blocks unauthorized access while allowing legitimate traffics.
    • Segmentation of network zones
      • Divides network into smaller, isolated zones to limit breach impacts
      • If one segment gets compromised, hackers can’t easily move laterally to another.
    • PLC-specific security appliances:
      • Added security features tailored specifically for the programmable logic controllers that are used by the Israeli facilities.
    • Continuous monitoring and logging of network traffic, user access logs, and system performance data.
      • Automated monitoring tools can be used to watch for suspicious behavior to allow quick response to threats
